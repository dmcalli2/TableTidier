Figure S2: Effect of anacetrapib on major coronary events among pre-specified categories of participants
Counts (and percentages) are of the participants with a particular baseline characteristic with a first major coronary event during follow-up.
Rate ratios comparing the outcome among participants assigned to anacetrapib with the outcome among those assigned to placebo are plotted. For individual baseline categories, rate ratios are plotted as squares, with the size of each square proportional to the amount of statistical information, and horizontal lines represent 95% confidence intervals (CIs). For the overall result, the rate ratio and its corresponding 95% CI is represented by a diamond. Tests of heterogeneity or trend (as appropriate) are shown for each baseline characteristic. For prior disease, participants may be in more than one prior disease category and these tests are of those with versus those without a history of the particular condition. Low-dose daily atorvastatin (which was taken by 50% of randomized participants) is defined as 10 mg in China and 20 mg elsewhere. High-dose daily atorvastatin (which was taken by the other 50% of randomized participants) is defined as 20 mg in China and 80 mg once daily elsewhere. Further details of the atorvastatin regimen and its usage have been reported previously.1 Waist:hip ratio is categorised as low (<0.87 in women; <0.94 in men); medium (≥0.87<0.93 in women; ≥0.94<1.00 in men); or high (≥0.93 in women; ≥1.00 in men). To convert cholesterol values to millimoles per liter multiply by 0.02586. To convert triglyceride values to millimoles per liter multiply by 0.01129. To convert apolipoprotein values to grammes per liter multiply by 0.01. Estimated Glomerular Filtration Rate is calculated using the CKD-EPI formula. Urine albumin:creatinine ratio is categorised as normo-albuminuria <26.5 mg/g (<3 mg/mmol); micro-albuminuria ≥26.5 <265 mg/g (≥3 <30 mg/mmol); or macro-albuminuria ≥265 mg/g (≥30 mg/mmol).
